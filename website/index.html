CH·02 — THE MECHANISM

Duplicate, race, dedup.

This is how seamless internet failover works on macOS: there's no clever switching to fail, because nothing switches. Both links carry a copy of every packet. The gateway keeps the first copy to arrive and discards the rest. Whichever link is healthy at that instant is the one that matters.

  1. 01

    Send it twice

    Your Mac copies each outbound packet and sends one over Wi-Fi and one over the phone's cellular link at the same moment.

  2. 02

    Let them race

    Both copies travel to the gateway by independent paths. They don't depend on each other, so a slow or dead link can't hold the other up.

  3. 03

    First copy wins

    The gateway forwards the first copy it sees and drops the duplicate. If a link goes quiet, its copies simply stop arriving — and you never notice.

CH·03 — WHY IT'S DIFFERENT

Built for staying up, not racing ahead.

Plenty of tools promise to bond links for more speed. This isn't that. Gemina VPN spends your second link on certainty — the connection holds when one path falters.

RELIABILITY, NOT SPEED

Survives a dropout

Because every packet already travels both links, there's no reconnect, no stall and no renegotiation when one drops. The healthy link was always carrying it too.

ANY ANDROID

Just plug in and tether

The app drives your phone's USB tether from userspace. Enable USB tethering and you're done. Any Android with tethering works; some phones are picked up natively by macOS.

NOTHING TO UNLOCK

No root, no kernel hacks

No rooting your phone. No kernel extensions, no DriverKit, no SIP changes on your Mac. It runs as an ordinary, unprivileged app.

CH·07 — STRAIGHT ANSWERS

Frequently asked questions

Is my Android phone supported?

If it can share its connection over USB tethering — and essentially every modern Android can — it works. There's no approved-device list. Some phones are also recognised natively by macOS, which makes setup even smoother.

Do I need to root my phone?

No. The app drives the standard USB tether that Android already provides. Nothing on the phone is unlocked, flashed or modified.

Does it change my Mac's security or SIP?

No. There are no kernel extensions, no DriverKit drivers and no System Integrity Protection changes. Gemina VPN runs as an ordinary, unprivileged macOS app.

Is my traffic encrypted?

Encryption is still in development, so we won't claim it yet. The dual-path transport — the same packet reaching the gateway over both links and being deduplicated — is proven and working. We'll be clear about the encryption story before general release.

Can I self-host the gateway?

Yes. The app and gateway are open source, and the gateway ships as a single container you can run on your own server. The hosted subscription simply means we keep that gateway online for you.

Will this make my connection faster?

No — and that's deliberate. It's a reliability tool, not a bandwidth one. The second link buys you a connection that survives a dropout, not a bigger pipe.
